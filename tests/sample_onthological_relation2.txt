Research Log — Distributed Training Incident & Recovery
Author: Lena Vasquez
Dates: 2025-11-03 through 2025-12-19

=== Entry 1 — November 3, 2025 ===

Started the full pre-training run for Meridian-7B on the Condor cluster (128x
NVIDIA H100 GPUs) at the Stockholm datacenter. Using DeepSpeed ZeRO Stage 3 with
activation checkpointing. Dataset is RedPajama-v2 filtered through our custom
toxicity classifier built by Tomás Herrera's safety team.

Hyperparameters: lr=3e-4, warmup=2000 steps, cosine decay to 1e-5, batch size
4096 tokens × 128 GPUs = 524,288 tokens/step. Estimated time to completion:
14 days. Total cost estimate: $380,000 on CoreWeave infrastructure.

Checkpoint frequency: every 1,000 steps to AWS S3 (us-west-2) with redundant
copy to Backblaze B2 in Amsterdam.


=== Entry 2 — November 7, 2025 ===

Loss curve looks healthy at step 12,400. Validation perplexity on the Pile
dropped from 18.2 to 11.7. Ran a quick eval on HellaSwag — 62.3% accuracy,
tracking with Llama-2-7B at the same token count.

Yuki Tanaka from the Evaluation team flagged that our MMLU scores are lagging
behind expectations. She suspects the math subset is pulling us down because
RedPajama-v2 underrepresents mathematical content. Recommended mixing in
OpenWebMath and the AMPS dataset.

Carlos Mendez approved the data mixture change. New composition:
- 70% RedPajama-v2
- 15% The Stack v2 (code)
- 10% OpenWebMath + AMPS
- 5% Wikipedia + BookCorpus

Paused training at step 15,000 to re-shard the dataset. Tomás Herrera re-ran
the toxicity filter on the new math data — 0.3% rejection rate, acceptable.


=== Entry 3 — November 12, 2025 ===

Resumed training with updated data mix. Immediate improvement: math validation
loss dropped 23% within 2,000 steps.

Incident report: At step 22,847, GPU node worker-gpu-089 threw an ECC
(Error-Correcting Code) memory error. DeepSpeed's elastic training module
detected the failure and redistributed the load across 127 remaining GPUs.
However, the automatic recovery corrupted the optimizer state for 3 of the
128 data-parallel ranks.

Root cause analysis by infrastructure lead Amara Osei:
- worker-gpu-089 had been flagged by Condor's health monitoring 48 hours prior
  with elevated temperature readings (87°C sustained).
- The CoreWeave team (contact: Jin Park, SRE) confirmed the node had a failing
  cooling fan assembly.
- Recommendation: implement pre-emptive node draining when temperature exceeds
  82°C for more than 30 minutes.

Recovery: rolled back to checkpoint at step 22,000 (847 steps lost ≈ $2,300
in compute waste). Restarted on 127 GPUs. Filed hardware replacement ticket
HW-2025-1847 with CoreWeave.


=== Entry 4 — November 19, 2025 ===

Crossed the 50,000-step milestone. Current metrics:
- Validation perplexity (Pile): 8.4
- HellaSwag: 71.2%
- MMLU (5-shot): 44.8% (up from 38.1% pre-data-mix-change)
- HumanEval (code): 28.7%

Yuki Tanaka's team ran the full LM Evaluation Harness. Meridian-7B is now
competitive with Mistral-7B-v0.1 on most benchmarks except TruthfulQA, where
we score 34.2% vs their 42.1%.

Discussion with Carlos Mendez and Professor Elena Rossi (University of
Edinburgh, our academic advisor) about the TruthfulQA gap. Professor Rossi
suggested that our safety filter might be too aggressive on controversial-but-
factual content, causing the model to hedge excessively. She recommended a
two-pass filtering approach: first pass for genuine toxicity, second pass
for factual calibration.

Amara Osei reported that the replacement GPU node (worker-gpu-091) is online.
Back to 128 GPUs. Also implemented the temperature-based pre-emptive drain
policy — committed to the Condor cluster config on GitHub (PR #4521).


=== Entry 5 — November 28, 2025 ===

Training complete at step 87,500. Final pre-training metrics:
- Pile perplexity: 6.8
- HellaSwag: 76.4%
- MMLU: 51.2%
- HumanEval: 34.1%
- TruthfulQA: 39.8% (improved after Professor Rossi's filter adjustment)
- WinoGrande: 73.9%
- ARC-Challenge: 52.7%

Total compute: 11.2 days (vs 14 estimated — the data mix change was net
positive). Final cost: $312,000 (under budget by $68,000).

Model artifacts uploaded to:
- Hugging Face Hub: nexus-ai/meridian-7b-base (private repo)
- Internal registry: models.nexus.internal/meridian/7b-v1-base

Post-training plan agreed with Carlos Mendez:
1. Supervised fine-tuning (SFT) on Nexus-Instruct-v3 dataset — Lena Vasquez
2. Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) — Tomás Herrera
3. Safety alignment via Constitutional AI — Professor Elena Rossi consulting
4. Quantization to GGUF for edge deployment — Yuki Tanaka


=== Entry 6 — December 5, 2025 ===

SFT phase started. Using the Nexus-Instruct-v3 dataset (520,000 instruction-
response pairs) curated by the Data Quality team in Toronto, led by Fatima
Al-Rashidi.

Issue: the SFT training on 32 GPUs hit an OOM (Out of Memory) error at batch
size 8 with sequence length 4096. Switched to gradient accumulation (effective
batch size 32, micro-batch 2) and enabled Flash Attention 2. Resolved.

Parallel track: Tomás Herrera began collecting preference data for DPO.
Partnered with Scale AI (contact: Lisa Nguyen) for human annotation. 50,000
preference pairs contracted, delivery by December 15.

Amara Osei's infrastructure team deployed a new monitoring dashboard in Grafana
tracking GPU utilization, memory pressure, and checkpoint I/O latency across
all Condor nodes.


=== Entry 7 — December 12, 2025 ===

SFT training complete after 3 epochs. Post-SFT evaluation:
- MT-Bench score: 7.2 / 10 (competitive with Llama-2-7B-Chat at 6.9)
- AlpacaEval 2.0 win rate: 14.3% (vs GPT-4 as reference)

Scale AI delivered 48,200 preference pairs (96.4% of contracted volume).
Lisa Nguyen explained the shortfall — 1,800 pairs flagged by their internal
QA for low inter-annotator agreement and removed.

Tomás Herrera started DPO training. Using the TRL library from Hugging Face.
Initial DPO hyperparameter search: β ∈ {0.1, 0.3, 0.5}, learning rate 5e-7.

Critical finding: Yuki Tanaka discovered that Meridian-7B-SFT has a bias toward
generating lists even when a narrative answer is more appropriate. Root cause
traced to the Nexus-Instruct-v3 dataset — 34% of responses use bullet-point
format. Fatima Al-Rashidi's team in Toronto is rebalancing the dataset for v4.

Professor Elena Rossi visited our London office for a 2-day workshop on
Constitutional AI alignment methodology. Key takeaway: the "red-teaming then
revise" loop should be done with at least 3 diverse evaluator personas to avoid
alignment monoculture.


=== Entry 8 — December 19, 2025 ===

DPO training complete. Final Meridian-7B-Chat metrics:
- MT-Bench: 7.8 / 10
- AlpacaEval 2.0 win rate: 18.1%
- TruthfulQA: 47.3%
- Safety eval (Nexus Red Team suite): 94.2% refusal rate on harmful prompts

Carlos Mendez approved the model for internal beta release. Deployment plan:
- January 6, 2026: Internal API launch on Kubernetes (managed by Amara Osei)
- January 13: Beta access for Nexus Technologies product teams
- February 1: Public release on Hugging Face Hub
- February 15: GGUF quantized versions (Q4_K_M, Q5_K_M, Q8_0) by Yuki Tanaka

Remaining risks flagged by Tomás Herrera:
1. The model occasionally generates plausible-sounding but fabricated citations.
   Mitigation: RAG integration with our document store (Project Athena).
2. Multilingual performance is weak — Meridian-7B was trained primarily on
   English. Future work: Meridian-7B-Multilingual with CC-100 data.

Filed patent application PA-2025-0892 for the two-pass toxicity filtering
method developed with Professor Rossi.

---
End of research log.
