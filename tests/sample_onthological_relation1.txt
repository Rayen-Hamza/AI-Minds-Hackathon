## 1. Project Atlas Update

Marcus Rivera presented the latest progress on Project Atlas. The team has migrated
the core inference engine from TensorFlow to PyTorch, reducing average latency from
120ms to 68ms. Priya Kapoor confirmed that the new ONNX export pipeline is working
in staging at the Berlin datacenter.

Key decisions:
- Atlas v2.3 release is scheduled for February 28, 2026.
- David Okonkwo will lead the integration with the Hermes API gateway.
- The deployment target is Amazon Web Services us-east-1, with failover to Google
  Cloud Platform europe-west4.

---

## 2. Research Collaboration with MIT CSAIL

Emily Zhang reported on the partnership with MIT's Computer Science and Artificial
Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL). Professor James Thornton's group has shared a
pre-print on sparse mixture-of-experts architectures that could reduce our model
size by 40% without accuracy loss.

Action items:
- Sarah Chen to schedule a follow-up call with Professor Thornton by January 25.
- Marcus Rivera to benchmark the MoE approach against our current dense transformer
  on the internal SQuAD-Finance dataset.
- Budget allocation of $150,000 for GPU compute on Lambda Cloud approved by CFO
  Robert Kim.

---

## 3. Competitive Analysis

Priya Kapoor shared intelligence on competitor activity:
- Anthropic released Claude 4 with improved reasoning capabilities last week.
- OpenAI is rumored to be launching a lightweight model for edge devices, similar
  to what Microsoft demonstrated at CES 2026 in Las Vegas.
- Google DeepMind published a paper on constitutional AI alignment that contradicts
  some of our safety assumptions.

David Okonkwo raised concerns about our position in the European market. The EU AI
Act enforcement begins March 1, 2026, and our compliance team in Dublin has flagged
three risk areas in the Atlas pipeline.

## 4. Hiring and Team Structure

Emily Zhang proposed restructuring the ML Engineering team:
- Create a new "Foundation Models" sub-team led by Marcus Rivera (4 headcount).
- Move the MLOps function under David Okonkwo's Infrastructure org.
- Hire a dedicated AI Safety researcher — posting on LinkedIn and reaching out to
  candidates from the Partnership on AI network.

Robert Kim approved a total hiring budget of $2.4M for Q1-Q2 across all AI teams,
with priority given to the Paris and Toronto offices.
